About me 02 / 07

From curiosity
to code
to discovery

My journey into bioinformatics started with a simple question: what if we could read the genome the way we read data? That question led me through a PhD where I combined deep learning with genomic prediction — working specifically on crossbreeding systems in dairy cattle, where the complexity of mixed genetics demands more than traditional statistical models.

I build tools that sit at the exact intersection of biology, mathematics, and software — because that's where the most interesting problems live.

Along the way I developed a deep fluency across the full stack of modern bioinformatics: from raw sequencing data and single-cell RNA-seq pipelines to ML model development, reproducible workflows, and scientific communication. I navigated peer review, oral presentations at EAAP, and research internships at Roslin Institute Edinburgh, INRAe, and Inserm.

Today I'm building OmicSage — a platform designed to make omics data analysis more accessible, reproducible, and powerful. I'm open to freelance collaborations and full-time roles where rigorous science meets real-world impact.
